Quantum Computing Concepts

What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computing is a type of computing that uses quantum physics principles to solve complex problems faster than traditional computers.

Qubit

A qubit is the basic unit of quantum computing. It can be 0, 1, or both at the same time.

Superposition

Superposition allows a qubit to exist in multiple states at once, increasing computing power.

Entanglement

Entanglement connects qubits so that changing one instantly affects another, even over long distances.

Quantum Gates

Quantum gates manipulate qubits to perform operations, similar to logic gates in classical computers.

Quantum vs Classical Computers

Classical computers use bits (0 or 1), while quantum computers use qubits for faster problem solving.

Applications

Used in cybersecurity, medicine, artificial intelligence, weather forecasting, and finance.

Future Scope

Quantum computing will revolutionize technology, research, and problem-solving in the future.
